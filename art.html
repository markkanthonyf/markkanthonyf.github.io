Rumumoric (2016)


When I was a kid, I would have never expected to be making an art portfolio. I was always, as embarassing as this may be, a theater kid. Of course, I always had a knack for doodling during class and wishing I could be in the design, visual arts, or clay-making classes, but it just never came to fruition.

This was until 2015 when I got my first iPad and pen. Was I good at drawing? Of course not, but it was such an interesting form of expression that I hadn't been able to utilize before. I drew fanart, original characters, scenes, monsters! You name it, I drew it. It was something that I truly grew to love.

This first piece was made in 2016. One of my friends had asked me to draw her character and I couldn't have been more excited. You could say, this was my first commission too. This was the first attempt of me putting everything I have learned over the year to use. Room for improvement, obviously, but I was proud and still am proud at where I started.

An Alien and His Ghost (2017)

Original Character

One of the best things to come with your art is your creativity. This is evident in the pieces I made and how much they've changed over time. Over the year between 2016 and 2017 I was beginning to explore different worlds and creatures of my own creation. This - the creation of life - of my own imagination - is what pulled me to art and animation. This is why I love it so much.

The second piece in this portfolio was made in 2017. Catch the theme yet? At first I wanted to make a robot and its soul. But the strange and beautiful thing about art and design is that its always prone to change. Unless it's for something professional, who's to tell you what you can or can't make? About half-way through the creation of this image, it changed from a lost robot to an honestly edgy looking alien. He wasn't tied to any story or world, just a simple character that I made. Of course, with his Ghost as well.


Sangria (2018)

Original Character

What's something an artist always learns in art theory? That's right, color theory!

My mortal enemy.

The problem with never taking professional art classes and learning how to draw yourself, is that you don't immediatley learn what looks good and what doesn't. It's all about trial and error. It takes a long time for a person to put to colors together and realize what'll catch a person's eye. Or what will genuinly make them sick. Somehow I did the latter quite a lot. Color theory was something I really needed to get better at.

This piece was done in 2018; making it one of the first artworks that I actually tried to do multiple professional things. 1) make something that's colors look good and 2) try a different brush type. I had gotten into a habit of using one kind of pen because I was convinced that's all I needed. But look at how much better this piece is from the last! Just because I didn't use the fine-pen.


The Goddess (2019)

Original Character

Up until now, everything I have made was from my own head. Nothing was copied (besides Rumumoric's design, of course); nothing was used to really push my artistic skill. This changed in 2019. I realized that in order for me to become a better artist, I needed to push myself outside my comfort zone. Really try something new. All my pieces up to this point had been simple head-shots or still body poses. Nothing more or less. That needed to change.

This was an art piece I made in 2019. What did I do that was so different than the others? Well, I studied someone. I actually observed how the human anatomy worked. Which, might I say, is something even more incredible in it of itself. If you're an artist, especially a character designer, you have to appreciate how beautiful the human body is. Anyway, I digress. I used a live video of a woman slowly moving into different poses. This is the one that spoke most to me, so I drew it. It has forever become one of my most favorite drawings I have ever made.


Power (2020)

Original Character

Of course, not everything is about digital art. In fact, it's not even my favorite media to use. My all time favorite is the simple pencil and paper. There's something so incredible in something that's so simple. Some of my better art works have all be on pencil and paper. The rigidess and roughness of the sketches brings out a kind of fascination that I can't get anywhere else.

This was made in 2020 during the height of the pandemic. I wanted to make a character so in love with power that he went insane. Personally, I think I did pretty well. This was also a piece I did to test my ability to draw the male anatomy. Still just a bust shot, but one of my prouder works.

Also, look at that dynamic hand! So good!


Willa Baker (2021)


One of the best things about art is that you get to see yourself grow. Just look at the difference between 2016 and now. Time and practice truly does wonders for artists, or truly anything that takes patience. Game Designers and the comprehencion of code, dancers becoming more comfortable in their body, singers making a stronger voice. It's all and alwasys will be about the time you take to practice.

This piece was a commission I did for a friend. This was when I took everything I learned: the color theory, media and pen strokes, the study of human anatomy, and patience, all into one.

Untitled (2022)

Original Character

We're finally up to date. But what's something I still haven't mentioned? Something that really should have been worked on years and years ago? Yep, that's right, shading. Guess what that ties into once again? Color theory! My hatred for it will never cease. But this was different. I knew I needed to work on it, so in this piece, I took my time to understand how the sun affects a person's face.

in 2022, I grew to appreciate what shapes different brushes created on a canvas. So, mixing the two together, I created this. Just like when I did my art study on anatomy, this has become one of my favorite art pieces I have ever made. There seems to be a theme between learning somethin new and testing it.

I wonder what I'll learn next.
